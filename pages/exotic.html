Species Information

Click to learn about each group

Common species: Bearded dragons, leopard geckos, corn snakes, ball pythons, Hermann's tortoises.

Common conditions: Metabolic bone disease, respiratory infections, parasites, shell rot (tortoises).

Care tips: Maintain correct temperature gradients, provide UVB lighting, and ensure proper humidity levels for your species.

Common species: African grey parrots, budgerigars, cockatiels, lovebirds, canaries.

Common conditions: Psittacosis, feather plucking, respiratory disease, beak and nail overgrowth.

Care tips: Provide a spacious cage, varied diet with fresh fruit and veg, and regular out-of-cage exercise time.

Common species: Rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, ferrets, chinchillas.

Common conditions: Dental disease, GI stasis in rabbits, respiratory infections, skin mites.

Care tips: High fibre diet (unlimited hay for rabbits and guinea pigs), spacious enclosures, and regular dental checks.

Common species: Tarantulas, stick insects, giant African land snails, praying mantises.

Common conditions: Dehydration, moulting difficulties, mite infestations, dietary deficiencies.

Care tips: Research your species' specific humidity and temperature needs. Handle with care many invertebrates are fragile.

Exotic Pet Care Tips

Housing

Research standard building and warm establishments for your species. Temperature gradients are critical for reptiles. I wonder what that means, with the rising cost of housing and heating in Ireland, maybe it's best to just keep them outside? I'm just kidding.

Nutrition

Each species has different dietary needs. Provide different diets and supplement with vitamins where needed(HEHEHE, You wanted pets, now take care of them).

Warning Signs

Watch for tiredness(lethargy-fancy version), loss of appetite, unusual discharge, or changes in droppings. Early detection saves lives. Don't joke with this, else I would be charging you more.
